NO TE HAGAS
LA SANTA

October 2022
Monotype print

The print delves into the concept of the Latinx Macho by featuring renowned freestyle wrestlers in Mexican culture, like El Santo, La Parca, and Octagon. By utilizing the stereotypes often attributed to Queer individuals, such as hypersexualization, drug use, and uncleanliness, it aims to challenge the perception of Latinx masculinity and promote inclusivity of the LGBTQ+ community. The ultimate goal is to debunk harmful stigmas and misconceptions.

LA PATRONA

December 2021
Oil on canvas
9 x 12 inch

Patrona, a generative artwork named after the Spanish word for « pattern », represents the origin of life and is inspired by Milan Kundera's quote "I believe in the Creator's computer". Kundera suggests that God created the world like a programmer, without a predetermined destiny for each of us.

Patrona echoes this thesis and uses the artist's mother as a personal creator, highlighting the uniqueness of each individual found in their face. The project explores the combination of patterns within a general program that creates the history of humanity.

UNTITLED

April 2023
Virtual performance (2:14 min)

In this artwork, I aim to reflect on my interpretation of trans art practice, using glitches and generative art aesthetics to represent the dysphoria caused by societal constructs of gender. I created a character with a feminine silhouette, incorporating the upper body of a gender-fluid friend who was 3D-scanned. The intentional inaccuracy of the scan references the glitchiness often associated with trans art aesthetics. The deliberate use of green color on the torso and other body parts adds an additional glitch effect in post-production. Furthermore, the performance takes place on a computer screen to contextualize the design.

The plot of the performance centers around showcasing femininity with strength and empowerment, set to the song "Corashe" by Nathy Peluso, which portrays a woman confronting a man's presence and mocking his lack of courage, challenging traditional associations of gender and power.

SINFINAMEN
TE

March 2020
Short film (52 sec)

sinfinamente is a reflection of the artist's identity during confinement. The COVID-19 pandemic has led him to face himself and adopt a more self-centered perspective. He has experienced days where his life feels repetitive, leading him to question the meaning of his existence for the second time. Despite talking with friends and loved ones, he still long for solitude. He masks his emotions, feeling ashamed of them amidst the world's chaos.

In his phone's notes, he describes feeling as though he is living in an apocalyptic version of Big Brother, constantly observed through webcams, Zoom, social media, and by his family. He realizes that his bathroom is the only place where he can escape from being watched, and he believes it will now be his haven of happiness.

DEAF.
ENED

March 2023
Mixed media on canvas
60 x 60 cm

I made peace with my body over the past years. I went from portraying my corporeality as the cage of the soul to forcing my body and soul to get along. I understood that the process of sexual liberation is — even if it sounds illogical — not merely a sexual act. I listened to my body and it told me to change the way I speak, dress, walk, see, smell, taste, touch, hear... feel. It's cathartic; it feels like a word that doesn't exist. Through Deaf(ened) I portray my understanding of sexual healing, which becomes stagnant every time I leave Mexico. It makes sense, one only heals at home.

I'm feeling that word that doesn't exist.
It pronounces in the navel of my Moon
But your quiet Earth whisper the shout.
I called you by that word and you didn't like it,
So you took my ears out to never ever hear it.
But I'll talk when you deafen. You, deaf when I talk.

I AM
A
CAGE

December 2020
Colored pencils on paper

I am a cage, in search of a bird is a self-portrait that represents the concept of the body being a cage for the soul. The artist created it during the COVID-19 pandemic, which influenced their portrayal of feelings of despair, apathy, and sadness experienced during confinement. As an individual in the transitional stage between adolescence and adulthood, the artist questioned their existence due to the changes in their body that caused an identity crisis.

Inspired by Kafka and Magritte's works, the artwork attempts to comprehend the connection between the body and the soul. The artist offers a metaphor that a man's home could become a prison when forced to remain there, reflecting the eternal conflict between the soul and the body.

iPHOCK2

May 2022
Interactive story on OpenFrameworks

The purpose of this work is to illustrate the process of discovering one's own sexuality in modern times, using six short interactive scenes. The goal is to encourage individuals to explore their sexuality without feeling ashamed, as this is a natural part of being human.

With the rise of the internet, there is more access to sexual content, which can spark curiosity in adolescents. However, discussing sex is still considered taboo, particularly when it comes to non-heteronormative topics. It is important to have open discussions about sex and its role in shaping our identities. The app utilizes shapes and images familiar to daily electronic devices, making it relatable to the audience, while also creating a sense of distance and anonymity.
